Design System Highlights for Decentralized Social Media for Web 3.

In our collaborative case study, our team implemented an enhanced design system for iOS, incorporating key elements such as brand guideline, typeface, color palette, iconography buttons, components, design patterns, layout grid and accessibility.

Typeface: We adopted the Lato typeface as a core element of our design system for iOS. Lato offers excellent legibility and a modern aesthetic, ensuring a visually appealing and user-friendly experience.

Color Palette: Our design system embraces a vibrant color palette, featuring shades of purple and blue. These colors were carefully chosen to evoke emotions of creativity, trust, and tranquility, creating a visually engaging and harmonious user interface.

Iconography: Following the principles of Apple's Human Interface Guidelines, we implemented a refined set of icons that are intuitive, recognizable, and consistent with iOS standards. These purposeful icons enhance usability and provide clear visual cues to guide users throughout the application.

Buttons: We utilized the standardized iOS buttons in our design system, ensuring familiarity and ease of use for iOS users. By adhering to the established button styles and interactions, we maintained a cohesive and seamless user experience.

Layout Grid: Our design system incorporates a 5-column layout grid with a 24-pixel margin and 24-pixel gutter. This grid system provides a structured framework for organizing content and maintaining consistent spacing and alignment across various screen sizes, promoting visual harmony and ease of navigation.

Components: Our design system includes a comprehensive library of reusable UI components, such as buttons, forms, cards, and navigation bars. These components follow consistent styling and behavior guidelines, enabling designers and developers to create cohesive and efficient user interfaces while reducing duplication of effort.

Design Patterns for iOS: Our design system incorporates specific design patterns tailored to iOS, following Apple's Human Interface Guidelines. These patterns provide best practices and standard solutions for common user interface challenges, ensuring familiarity and consistency for iOS users and promoting a seamless user experience.

Accessibility Guidelines: Accessibility is a fundamental aspect of our design system. We have implemented comprehensive accessibility guidelines, ensuring our designs are inclusive and accessible to all users, including those with disabilities. These guidelines address factors such as color contrast, text size, keyboard navigation, and screen reader compatibility, providing an inclusive user experience for everyone.

LO-FI Design

The lo-fi design of our application focuses on the foundational elements of our design system, providing a simplified and stripped-down representation of the user interface. Using basic shapes and minimal details, the lo-fi design allows us to quickly iterate and test key interactions and layout concepts.

HI-FI Design

The hi-fi design of our application builds upon the foundation established in the lo-fi design phase, bringing in refined visual elements and enhanced details. With a focus on delivering a polished and engaging user experience, the hi-fi design showcases the full potential of our design system. In the hi-fi design, we introduce vibrant colors from our carefully selected palette, incorporating shades of purple and blue to evoke emotions of creativity and trust.
