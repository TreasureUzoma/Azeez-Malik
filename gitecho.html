Gitecho: Connecting Talent and Technology

About

GITECHO, a new player in the market, seeks to compete with the well-established companies, in which those companies hold significant market shares and strong brand reputation. In today's rapidly evolving digital landscape, where technology disrupts traditional sectors, endless possibilities arise for individuals to pursue their true passions and make a positive impact on the world. GITECHO aims to address this challenge by targeting the Gen-Z market (18-25) and focusing on innovative tech sectors, enabling young individuals to leverage technology in their chosen fields and contribute to meaningful change.

Problem Statement

GITECHO faces the challenge of establishing itself and competing against existing companies in the job search market. GITECHO aims to overcome this hurdle by targeting the Gen-Z market (18-25) and focusing on innovative tech sectors, providing a platform that enables young individuals to leverage technology in their chosen fields and make a positive impact on the world.

Goals

Our objective is to address this challenge by concentrating on the Gen-Z (18-25) market and catering specifically to the innovative tech sectors, which are currently underserved by existing companies. GITECHO distinguishes itself by delivering an enhanced user experience and interface that takes into account the unique needs of our target audience. This includes streamlining the job search process, providing personalized recommendations, ensuring privacy and data security, facilitating seamless communication and engagement, and implementing a continuous process of testing and iteration.

Competitors Analysis

A competitor analysis helps us learn the ins and outs of how our competition works.

OTTA

Otta is a similar platform that allows users to find and book tech talent. They have a larger user base than Gitecho, and they offer a more comprehensive set of features.

TOPTAL

Toptal is a more exclusive platform that only matches users with top-tier tech talent. They have a higher price point than Gitecho, but they offer a more guaranteed level of quality.

Key Takeaways

Gitecho needs to focus on differentiating themselves from their competitors. They can do this by offering unique features or services that their competitors don't offer. For example, they could offer a more personalized onboarding experience for new users, or they could offer a wider range of tech talent to choose from.

Gitecho needs to improve their website's user experience. The website is currently a bit clunky and difficult to navigate. They could make it easier for users to find the information they're looking for, and they could make it easier for users to book tech talent.

Gitecho needs to increase their marketing efforts. They need to get the word out about their platform and attract more users. They could do this by running paid advertising campaigns, or they could partner with other tech companies to promote their platform.

Solutions


The design landing screen includes a Strong and Bold headline that perfect describe the websites.

I used illustration in the hero section as it depicts what the websites is all about.

It can take users as little as 50milliseconds to form an opinion about a websites. An Illustration can do this far more quickly than a block of text.

I used the brand color which are purple and blue for the background of the hero section, white background and the branding colour for the rest of the screens.

These colors were carefully chosen to evoke emotions of creativity, trust, and tranquility, creating a visually engaging and harmonious user interface.


The redesign webpage had many different sections and was of good scroll length. Therefore, to avoid the webpage getting monotonous for the visitor, no two consecutive sections had the same layout. This kept the user glued to the web page for a longer duration when scrolling.

Adequate amount of breathing space and white space was given to every block.
