Designing an Improved "TailorMe" App for User Convenience.

Project Overview

This project aims to create a mobile app that allows customers to order and receive tailored clothes from the comfort of their homes. The app streamlines the ordering process, and delivery is made to the customer's doorstep. The app will be designed to make the process of ordering and receiving stitched clothes more convenient for customers by allowing them to place orders from their mobile devices and have the finished product delivered to their doorstep.

User Research

User research of the e-tailoring mobile delivery app involves gathering insights into the target audience's needs, preferences, and behaviors. This research includes conducting surveys, interviews, and focus groups to understand the users' pain points, their fashion choices, and how they interact with mobile devices. The research also entails analyzing user data to identify patterns and trends, including how they engage with fashion-related content on social media. These findings inform the app's design and development, ensuring it caters to the users' specific needs and preferences, resulting in a successful and satisfying experience for the end-users.


1 Pain Point

Inconvenience in ordering: Many customers find it inconvenient to visit the tailor physically due to their busy schedules. This pain point can be addressed by designing an app that allows customers to order for tailored clothes from their mobile devices, and at their own convenience.

2 Pain Point

Late deliveries: Many tailors disappoint their clients by not meeting up with delivery dates. To address this pain point, the app can include a tracking feature that allows customers to monitor the progress of their orders and know exactly when to expect delivery.

3 Pain Point

Limited access to fabric and styles: Customers may find it difficult to access a variety of fabrics and styles due to their location or limited options from local tailors. To address this pain point, the app can provide a wide range of fabric and style options to choose from.

4 Pain Point

Poor communication and understanding of customer needs: Many tailors do not understand their customer's needs, leading to poor communication and unsatisfactory end products. The app can address this pain point by incorporating clear communication channels.
